About Me

Building structures.
Writing code. Teaching both.

I'm a civil engineer and master's student at Pulchowk Campus (IOE), specialising in the seismic performance of reinforced concrete structures in Nepal's high-risk Zone V environment.

My work sits at the intersection of structural analysis and software — I build Python-based desktop tools that automate design checks per IS 456 and IS 13920, reducing manual effort and human error in everyday engineering workflows.

Before grad school I worked at MATE Engineering Solutions on building design and a bamboo panel prefabrication research project with Habitat for Humanity International. I've also taught undergraduate structural engineering at Hillside College of Engineering, and I believe that communicating engineering clearly is just as important as doing it well.

I speak Nepali, English, Hindi, and Maithali.

Selected Work

Projects

01
Software Tool · Open Source
Beam Design Pro

A full-featured Python/Tkinter desktop application for reinforced concrete beam design per IS 456:2000. Supports singly and doubly reinforced beams with a wizard-style workflow, live validation, and professional reporting.

  • Singly & doubly reinforced beam design (IS 456:2000)
  • Exposure condition → min. cover & concrete grade (Cl. 26.4)
  • Factored moment, Mulim, Ast1, Ast2, Asc calculations
  • Deflection check with modification factor kt
  • Moment of resistance (MuR) verification
  • Auto-generated design summary report (exportable .txt)
Python 3 Tkinter NumPy IS 456:2000 RC Design
